About Me

Hi! My name is Qadir Ali I am a computer science graduate who who wants to increase his knowledge on different programming languages.


Aside from coding, I love playing basketball and video games! I spend a lot of time at the park practicing my moves and if not my headphones are on and I’m in a virtual world.


I also like to spend my time helping out at the local mosque, whether that’s fund-raising or collecting food for the poor.


I also have a small 100% electric last mile delivery business, I believe that electric technology is the way forward for the climate and transport going forward.

Why FAC?

I came across FAC when a sibling of mine joined and graduated at FAC, after hearing overwhelming praise for the course and the support she received from transitioning with no coding experience to a fully fledged developed is what drove me to apply.


FAC’s focus on peer-led learning and community is what stands out to me the most. I feel that I can learn a lot from the course because I believe it will help and push me to take my coding to the next level.


I love being able to exchange ideas with people and I believe FAC will allow me to do just that. I also admire that I will be mentored by someone who just recently completed the same course I will be sitting. The understanding shown when taught like this would be unlike how I was taught at university. On the other hand the fact that once I have completed I will also be able to mentor the next cohort and have an impact on their development will be very rewarding.

Why Coding?

I have been coding since I did my degree in 2014. I love figuring out how new technologies work and have been doing so since then.


I have a lot of interest in cyber security and the languages and technologies that are behind websites and software. I enjoy learning about the psychology aspect of a human, how and why they think in a certain way and if you combine that with software you get the basis of cybersecurity and why people try and “hack”.


I am always finding new rabbit holes to go down and get lost in, the internet is a huge pool of knowledge and I use that alongside my curiosity to challenge myself and fail repeatedly in order to learn new things which will help progress my career.
